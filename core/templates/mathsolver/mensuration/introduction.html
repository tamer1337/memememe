Mensuration

Mensuration is the branch of mathematics that studies the measurement of geometric figures and their parameters like length, volume, shape, surface area, lateral surface area etc.

Difference between 2D and 3D shapes

2D Shapes

  • 1. If a shape is surrounded by three or more straight lines in a plane, then it is a 2D shape
  • 2. These shapes have no depth or height
  • 3. These shapes have only two dimensions
  • 4. We can measure their area and perimeter

3D Shapes

  • 1. If a shape is surrounded by surfaces or planes then it is a 3D shape
  • 2. Unlike 2D, they have height or depth
  • 3. These shapes have three dimensions
  • 4. We can measure their volume, CSA, LSA or TSA

Important Terminologies

Terms Unit Definition
Area (A) m² or cm² Surface which is covered by the closed shape
Perimeter (P) m or cm The measure of the continuous line along the boundary of the given figure
Volume (V) m³ or cm³ The space occupied by a 3D shape
Curved Surface Area (CSA) m² or cm² If there is a curved surface, then total area is called CSA
Lateral Surface Area (LSA) m² or cm² The total area of all lateral surfaces that surround the given figure
Total Surface Area (TSA) m² or cm² The sum of all curved and lateral surface areas
